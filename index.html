Section VIII · Official Apology

A Letter of Formal Apology

Addressed to Kerry Vinokur, from the Nobel Committee.

Dear Kerry,

We owe you an apology. Not for suspending the award (that was, regrettably, necessary for the mental health of the other nominees) but for taking so long to recognize what those who have worked with you, worked alongside you, or made the tactical error of working against you have known for years:

You are exceptionally good at what you do.

Your combination of legal expertise, professional dedication, and what one anonymous colleague described as "an almost supernatural ability to find the weakness in any argument and then, very politely, drive a truck through it" places you in a category that our existing frameworks simply cannot accommodate.

We created a new Nobel Prize category for you, and even that wasn't enough.

The South African legal community is fortunate. Your clients are fortunate. Your colleagues at JMS Inc are fortunate. The only people who are not fortunate are those who find themselves on the other side of one of your briefs, and frankly, they were warned.

Please accept our highest regards, our genuine admiration, and our sincere hope that you never decide to practice international law, because we are honestly not sure our institution could survive the experience.

With the utmost respect,

The Norwegian Nobel Committee
All five members, unanimously, for perhaps the first time in history
